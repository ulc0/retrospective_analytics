scispaCy is a renowned and much-celebrated library among the biomedical, scientific, and clinical research community due to an impressive set of Named Entity Recognition(NER) models it incorporates. The fact that this is derived from the spaCy, a widely used open-source package makes it more appealing to a wider cohort compared to the other licensed/paid libraries.

Clinical literature mostly contains ambiguities as it consists of various abbreviations, clinical terms, temporal events, and biological entities which may resemble simple English terms lexicographic-ally. Because of this, specialized models trained on accomplished clinical databases are highly preferred nowadays. Some of the offerings from this library are discussed in different sections as follows.

Starting from the first principles the library installation is as follows :

Named Entity Recognition

Named Entity Recognition (NER) is of foremost importance as it is one major component of Natural Language Processing pipelines with healthcare and medical researchers dealing with vast amounts of texts, articles, and clinical notes. Below are the models trained on different global datasets from distinguished research labs, and each of these is available with the en_core_sci_sm and en_core_sci_md models.

Table 1. Various models and their respective entities from scispaCy

An example with the ‘en_ner_craft_md’ model is represented below -

import scispacy
import spacy
nlp = spacy.load("en_ner_craft_md")
text = "In this study, we aimed to characterise peripheral endothelial function using postocclusive reactive hyperaemia peripheral arterial tonometry (RH-PAT) in PCS patients following mild to moderate COVID-19. In addition, we analysed several endothelial biomarkers including Endothelin-1 (ET-1), Angiopoietin-2 (Ang-2) and Endocan (ESM-1) which play an important role in inflammatory and noninflammatory diseases associated with ED [18,19,20,21,22]. Furthermore, we assessed Interleukin 8 (IL-8), Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme (ACE) and ACE2. IL-8 is secreted not only by monocytes/macrophages but also by endothelial cells and vascular smooth muscle cells. This chemokine plays an important role in (endothelial) inflammation and regulation of leukocyte rolling as well as vascular permeability [23]. ACE and ACE2 are crucial actors in the maintenance of blood pressure and vascular homeostasis [2]. ACE converts angiotensin I into the vasoconstrictive angiotensin II, which activates angiotensin II receptors. Counterregulatory ACE2 cleaves angiotensin I into angiotensin 1–9 and metabolises angiotensin II to angiotensin 1–7. Peptides generated by ACE2 are ligands of the receptor Mas, which triggers protective, vasodilative signalling"
doc = nlp(text)

Entity Extraction

The major utility of this package is the extraction of specific entities related to clinical and biological domains using trained entity recognizers on various standard databases. After extraction, we can visualize them along with their tags using the custom options we set.

ent_df = pd.DataFrame()
for ent in doc.ents:
   ent_df = ent_df.append({'entity':ent.text,'label':ent.label_ },ignore_index = True)
   print(ent_df)
# custom colors and visualization
colors = {'GGP': 'orange', 'GO': 'lavender', 'CHEBI':'yellow', 'SO':'cyan', 'CL':'lightgreen'}
options = {'ents': ['GGP', 'GO', 'CHEBI', 'SO', 'CL'], 'colors':colors}
from spacy import displacy
displacy.render(doc, style='ent', jupyter=True, options=options)

These represent various entities like GGP — Gene or Gene Product, SO — Sequence Ontology, CL- Cell Line, etc. which give rich insights into the document content, enabling easier relationship identifications. These entities are annotated based on the CRAFT corpus.

Similarly, we can employ different models as discussed above according to the objective or problem in hand.

POS Tagging & Dependency Parsing

Part-of-Speech tagging allows one to identify the structure of a sentence by categorizing the words based on the definition of a word and its context.

On the other hand, Dependency Parsing helps one to identify the semantic relationships between the words in a sentence. This process enables one to screen out the required entities much more easily.

Both of these have methods developed by training them on the GENIA 1.0 corpus and on many PubMed texts, abstracts, and their metadata including the related MeSH (Medical Subject Headings) categories. To increase the robustness of these taggers/annotators, OntoNotes 5.0 was used which includes the semantic & syntactic elements.

a) Dependency Parsing

displacy.render(next(doc.sents), style='dep', jupyter=True, options={'distance': 130})

b) POS Tagging

for token in doc:
    print(f'{token.text:{10}} {token.pos_:{7}} {token.dep_:{7}} {spacy.explain(token.dep_)}')

Abbreviation Detection

Another important part that comes into play is the abbreviations that we often find in the biomedical literature, which vary highly depending on the instances compared to the normal scenarios. This utility serves to be a wonderful solution to this case.

from scispacy.abbreviation import AbbreviationDetector
# Add the abbreviation pipe to the spacy pipeline.
 nlp.get_pipe("abbreviation_detector")
 print("Abbreviation", "\t", "Definition")

Entity Linking

This function allows one to connect the terms extracted from the text to the closest concepts in the knowledge bases like UMLS, GO, MeSH, HPO, and RxNorm using the Nearest Neighbours approach. This process makes the medical concept identification more reliable and standardized according to the established hierarchies. The comparison between these different term references will also help deduce meaningful correlations.

Hearst Patterns

They are the most popular patterns used to extract the hypernym relations from the corpora, which are ubiquitously seen across the medical literature. This helps in Information Extraction, Curation of Datasets, and Taxonomical/Hierarchical relationship predictions. Simply, it helps identify spread terms and groups thereby reducing lexicographical misunderstandings.

For example, the Coronavirus is a category of viruses of man/mammals coming under disease hosts.

from scispacy.hyponym_detector import HyponymDetector
nlp.add_pipe("hyponym_detector", last=True, config={"extended": False})

Conclusion

With all these features of this package, one can easily perform a detailed analysis of the article and get a comprehensive structure of the literature. Moreover, we can extend the horizon of our understanding by relating these findings with databases and comparing them with the findings from similar tools.

- scispaCy is highly efficacious by leveraging its productive parent library spaCy’s functionalities.

- Offers intuitive visualizations to represent the biological relationships better, which is otherwise in-explainable and in-discernible from a one-dimensional biological text.

- The wide range of categorical entities it uses makes it even more valuable than other libraries.
